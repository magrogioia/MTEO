The MTEO Project

Project Overview

This project explores how semantic web technologies can enhance the management of complex marine ecosystems in aquariums. While modern aquariums rely on advanced sensors to continuously monitor physical parameters such as temperature, salinity, pH, and water chemistry, a significant portion of the knowledge needed to maintain ecosystem stability remains implicit and depends on the long-term experience of staff members.

This raised questions about how to enhance the semantic web. While comprehensive ontologies already exist that describe relationships between species and their characteristics, they primarily focus on taxonomic classification rather than elucidating their ecological roles in nature. Darwin Core, a derivative of Dublin Core, serves as a prime example of such ontologies.

For this particular project, the Genoa Aquarium "Tropical Seas" tank was analyzed, as it contains various species performing both simple and complex ecological functions.

Why choose this approach?

Traditional aquarium management often relies on tacit knowledge acquired through years of practical experience. This project emphasizes that by capturing this knowledge in a structured ontology, it becomes possible to support both human and software agents in understanding and reasoning about the system. The approach enables new staff, researchers, and educators to access detailed insights about ecological roles, functional interactions, and potential effects of interventions within the tank without the need to rely solely on experiential learning.

By focusing on interactions and functional contributions rather than mere taxonomy, the system provides a foundation for advanced queries, decision-making support, and educational applications. For example, one could determine which species regulate nutrient levels, which species are endangered, or how the removal or introduction of a particular organism might affect the overall balance of the ecosystem.

The MTEO Ontology

The Marine Tank Ecosystem Ontology (MTEO) complements existing standards such as Darwin Core and Schema.org, providing a lightweight, domain-specific semantic layer for aquarium ecosystems. It allows the representation of functional roles, species interactions, and ecological processes in a machine-readable format while remaining interpretable by humans. The ontology supports structured reasoning, enabling applications to perform complex queries, infer relationships, and assist in training and decision-making processes.

Applying MTEO to the Tropical Seas tank demonstrates the practical benefits of formalizing operational knowledge. All organisms, their roles, and interactions are represented in a structured, reusable manner. This approach reduces reliance on tacit knowledge, promotes knowledge transfer, and ensures that valuable ecological insights are preserved for both current and future aquarium management efforts.

The Ontology, and the example turtle dataset are available to download at this OpenSource Github Repository.

JSON-LD Structured Data

This HTML file contains embedded JSON-LD structured data describing the "Tropical Seas" aquarium tank and its ecosystem. The data includes all organisms, their ecological roles, conservation status, and functional interactions as defined by the MTEO ontology.

To view the JSON-LD data, right-click on the page → "View Page Source" (or press Ctrl+U) and search for <script type="application/ld+json">. The structured data is located in the <head> section and can be validated using Google's Rich Results Test.
